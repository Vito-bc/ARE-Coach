# ADA 2010 Standards — Accessible Routes
The minimum clear width of an accessible route is 36 inches. It may reduce to 32 inches
minimum at a point, for a maximum depth of 24 inches (for example, at a doorway). Passing
spaces are required at intervals on routes narrower than 60 inches so two wheelchairs can pass.

# ADA 2010 Standards — Ramps
The maximum running slope of a ramp on an accessible route is 1:12. The maximum rise for any
single ramp run is 30 inches. Ramps with a rise greater than 6 inches require handrails on both
sides. A level landing at least 60 inches long is required at the top and bottom of each run,
and where a ramp changes direction the landing must be at least 60 by 60 inches.

# ADA 2010 Standards — Reach Ranges
For an unobstructed forward or side reach, the maximum high reach is 48 inches above the finish
floor and the minimum low reach is 15 inches. Where the reach is over an obstruction, the maximum
high reach is reduced depending on the depth of the obstruction.

# ADA 2010 Standards — Turning Space and Maneuvering Clearance
A wheelchair turning space is either a circular space 60 inches in diameter or a T-shaped space
within a 60-inch square. Doorways require maneuvering clearances on each side that depend on the
approach direction (front, hinge, latch) and the door type.

# ADA 2010 Standards — Toilet Rooms
In an accessible single-user toilet room, fixtures and clearances must allow a 60-inch turning
space. The water closet centerline is 16 to 18 inches from the side wall, and grab bars are
required on the rear and side walls at specified lengths and heights.
